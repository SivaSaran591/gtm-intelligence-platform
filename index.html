Platform Logic

One intelligence loop,
four connected layers.

Each module operates independently but feeds the next. Market signals become campaigns. Campaigns generate deals. Deals inform partner performance.

01 · SIGNAL
Detect & Prioritise
SignalOps identifies accounts showing expansion signals, funding events, and ERP fit. A multi-dimensional TAL scoring model surfaces the right accounts at the right moment.
02 · ACTIVATE
Build & Launch
CampaignForge converts prioritised signals into targeted GTM programs. AI-generated personas, messaging, and channel strategy — ready to execute.
03 · MANAGE
Score & Forecast
DealLens monitors every deal in the pipeline for risk signals, engagement gaps, and forecast confidence — with recommended actions for each seller.
04 · SCALE
Track & Optimise
PartnerPulse measures partner pipeline contribution, performance trends, and QBR readiness — closing the loop from signal to ecosystem impact.

I build scalable decision systems for revenue teams.

My work focuses on helping mid-market organisations translate market signals into clear GTM actions. At SAP India, I operate as a single systems owner supporting the India Market Unit, designing structures that connect market signals, marketing execution, pipeline visibility, and partner ecosystems into one operational loop.

My approach is built on three principles. Scale — systems must work across an entire market unit, not a single campaign. Accountability — every signal should connect to pipeline progression or revenue movement. Integrity — decisions should be driven by real signals, not convenient narratives.

Most revenue teams today have strong data infrastructure but lack an intelligence layer that converts signals into decisions. The GTM Intelligence Platform is my attempt to close that gap.

I see my role not as a campaign executor but as a decision intelligence layer for revenue teams — translating scattered signals into clear operational direction.

I build systems. Not because the tools are interesting — though they are — but because a well-designed system changes how people make decisions. The four modules in this platform exist because I kept running into the same gap: good data, no intelligence layer on top of it.
